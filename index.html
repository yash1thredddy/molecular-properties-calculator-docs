What this tool does

Think of it as a lab assistant for molecules on paper. You give it a structure — typed in or uploaded in a spreadsheet — and it instantly reports the physical and chemical traits that decide whether a compound has a chance of becoming a drug.

Everything chemists usually calculate by hand or across several programs is gathered in one place: size, greasiness, how a molecule might cross membranes, whether it looks "drug-like," whether it could fool a screening assay, how efficiently it binds for its size, and how compounds group together across a whole library.

Who this is for

Medicinal chemists, screening scientists, and drug-discovery researchers. You do not need to know how it works under the hood — this guide explains the meaning, not the machinery, and defines every term as it appears.

What you can find out, at a glance

Is it drug-like?

Size, greasiness, and the classic "Rule of Five" — the quick gut-check every chemist runs first. See rules →

Will it fool my assay?

Interference flags that warn when a "hit" might be a false alarm. See flags →

How do compounds relate?

Charts, regressions, and natural groupings across a whole set. See visuals →

The five workspaces

The app is organized into five "modes," chosen from the menu inside the app. Each answers a different question — and each has a dedicated page in this guide.

Single Molecule

Paste one structure, get a full property breakdown plus a picture of the molecule.

Batch Processing

Upload a spreadsheet of thousands of molecules and calculate everything at once.

Data Visualization

Turn a dataset into interactive charts to spot relationships and outliers.

3D Regression

Fit a plane through three properties to study structure–activity relationships.

GMM Analysis

Let the tool discover the natural groups hiding in your data — without you defining them in advance.
